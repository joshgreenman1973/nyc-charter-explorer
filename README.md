# NYC Charter Explorer

A searchable, sortable web version of the **Charter of the City of New York**, plus a clean single-file export for use with Google NotebookLM.

**Live site:** https://joshgreenman1973.github.io/nyc-charter-explorer/

## What's here

- **Display options.** A theme toggle (auto / light / dark — respects your system setting by default), a compact-density toggle, and, on small screens, a "Filters" button that collapses the advanced filters into a drawer. All preferences persist in the browser.
- **`index.html`** — the explorer. Full text of the Charter (770 sections, 77 chapters), full-text + title search with relevance ranking, sort by chapter / section number / length / relevance, per-chapter filtering, sticky chapter table of contents. Data is bundled in `charter-data.js`, so the page also works by double-clicking the file locally — no server required.
- **`NYC-Charter-for-NotebookLM.md`** / **`.txt`** — the entire Charter as one structured document (table of contents + every section under its chapter). ~318,000 words, under NotebookLM's 500k-word-per-source limit. Drop either file into a NotebookLM notebook as a source.
- **`build.py`** — regenerates `charter-data.json`, `charter-data.js`, and the NotebookLM files from the raw source in `data/`.
- **`refresh.py`** + **`refresh/`** — the refresh pipeline. Re-pulls the Charter from American Legal Publishing and rebuilds everything. See "Keeping it current" below.

## Methodology

- **Source.** Text comes from the American Legal Publishing code library (https://codelibrary.amlegal.com/codes/newyorkcity/latest/overview), compiled via the open [BetaNYC nyc-charter-laws-rules](https://github.com/BetaNYC/nyc-charter-laws-rules) dataset (`data/index/json/charter.json`).
- **Currency.** The "current through" line shown on the page and in the NotebookLM document is read from the data (`data/versions.json`), not hardcoded, so it always reflects the last refresh. As of the latest refresh: current through Local Law 2026/094, enacted May 16, 2026, including amendments effective through May 27, 2026.
- **Keeping it current.** The Charter changes on a rolling basis as the Council passes legislation (some changes go through the Charter Revision process; many are ordinary Local Laws). American Legal Publishing republishes the full code as a bulk XML download as those changes land. `refresh.py` is the batch process that keeps this explorer in sync:
  1. `refresh/fetch-charter.js` downloads the authoritative Charter bulk XML zip from American Legal Publishing (`files.amlegal.com/pdffiles/NewYorkCity/Charter/XML.zip`).
  2. `refresh/build-charter.js` parses it into `data/charter.json` using BetaNYC's exact `fast-xml-parser` logic — verified to reproduce the original data byte-for-byte, so citations (and therefore the Charter Revision Commission map) stay stable.
  3. `refresh.py` copies the result in, refreshes `data/versions.json`, and runs `build.py`. It prints `CHANGED` or `UNCHANGED` so a scheduler can decide whether to redeploy, and aborts rather than overwrite if the fetch returns an implausibly small dataset.
  Run `python3 refresh.py` to refresh and rebuild, or `python3 refresh.py --check` to see whether the source has moved without writing anything. The Charter Revision Commission tagging (`data/crc-map.json`) is curated separately and is not affected by a refresh.
- **Change history.** When a refresh actually advances the version, `refresh.py` prepends a dated entry to [`CHANGELOG.md`](CHANGELOG.md) recording the old and new "current through" strings and — by comparing the new data against the prior copy section by section — whether any section text changed or it was only an effective-date bump. This gives a transparent, auditable record of when the Charter moved, mirroring the upstream [BetaNYC changelog](https://github.com/BetaNYC/nyc-charter-laws-rules/blob/main/CHANGELOG.md).
- **Automatic weekly refresh.** A scheduled task (`~/.claude/scheduled-tasks/charter-refresh`) runs every Monday at ~7 AM. It calls `refresh/auto-refresh.sh`, which refreshes, and only commits and pushes when the source actually changed (restoring the working tree on no-op runs). The script prints a single `RESULT:` line; on a real change the task adds a Google Calendar reminder to re-upload the file to NotebookLM (since NotebookLM does not auto-pull). Charter changes are infrequent, so most weeks are silent no-ops. The task runs while the app is open, or on next launch if it was closed when due.
- **Counts.** The source has 854 charter records: 84 chapter headers, 768 numbered sections, and 2 front-matter items (Preamble, Introductory). The explorer indexes the 770 text-bearing items (768 sections + 2 front-matter) grouped into the 77 chapters that contain at least one section. Eleven fully-repealed, empty chapters carry no section text and are omitted.
- **Citation fix.** The raw source truncates sub-chapter citations at the hyphen — e.g. "Chapter 18-" is shared by Chapters 18-A, 18-B, 18-C and 18-D. The canonical citation is recovered from each chapter's full heading ("Chapter 18-A: Civilian Complaint Review Board"), so sub-chapters stay distinct and sort correctly (Chapter 2 before 2-A, etc.).
- **Ordering.** Chapters and sections are ordered by their charter number, not the arbitrary order of the source index.
- **Amendment dating & spotlight.** Each section's editor's note is parsed for Local Law citations (`L.L. YYYY/NNN`). From these the explorer derives a most-recent **amendment year** (the Local Law *enactment* year), a distinct **amendment count**, and the full list of Local Laws on record. This powers the "Most recently amended" and "Most amended" sorts, the "amended since" filter, and the per-section badge. A separate "eff. YYYY" tag marks a change with a *future* effective date (parsed from calendar dates in the note) that is on record but not yet in force. 388 of 770 sections carry a Local Law note.
  - **Note — the Local Law dates above are not Charter Revision Commission attribution.** The source compiler records changes by Local Law number, not by which body originated them. The separate CRC tagging below fills that gap from official commission documents.
- **Charter Revision Commission tagging.** A separate layer (`data/crc-map.json`) tags sections changed by a *passed* ballot proposal from the 2018, 2019, 2024 and 2025 Charter Revision Commissions. This powers the "CRC" badge, the per-section proposal list, and the "revision commission" filter.
  - **How it was built.** Section lists come from each commission's official **"Proposed Charter Amendment Text"** appendix (the legal text that states "Section X … is amended/added/repealed"). Those enacting clauses were extracted programmatically and spot-verified against the source PDFs; the abstracts and ballot pamphlets contain no section numbers and were not used for the lists. Source URLs are in `data/crc-map.json`.
  - **Action per section (added / amended / repealed).** Each tagged section carries the action the proposal took on it. These come from the official text — primarily the § 1152 effective-date/transition clauses, which explicitly enumerate which sections each proposal is "amending", "adding" or "repealing" — cross-checked against the operative enacting clauses. A section that pre-existed and had a new subdivision added to it is recorded as **amended**; only a wholly new section is **added**. Shown in each section's "Charter Revision Commission changes" panel.
  - **Only passed proposals are tagged.** Proposals that failed at the ballot (2024 Q6 MWBE/modernization; 2025 Q6 even-year elections) did not change the Charter and are recorded with `passed: false` but not applied as tags. The footer's "Proposed but rejected by voters" panel lists them and the sections they would have changed, for context only. § 1152 (the Charter's effective-date/transition section) is amended by nearly every proposal.
  - **Confidence.** Section lists and actions: high (stated verbatim in official CRC amendment text). Pass/fail outcomes: from election results. This covers the four most recent commissions only — earlier amendments are reflected in the Local Law dating but not CRC-tagged.
- **Cross-reference links.** In a section's body text, numeric references to other Charter sections ("§ 197-d", "section 1152") are linked to jump to and open the target. References that don't resolve to a section in this dataset (e.g. citations to other laws, or section numbers spelled out in words) are left as plain text.
- **Shareable views.** The current search, filters and sort are reflected in the page URL, so a particular view can be shared and reopened.
- **Share a section or passage.** Each section has a "share" control that opens Copy link / Email / Tweet options for a direct deep link to that section. If you first select some text within a section, the share menu adds a "Selected passage" row whose links carry the passage; opening such a link jumps to the section and highlights that exact passage for the recipient. (Passage links use a custom `#sec_…&p=…` fragment that the page resolves itself, so it works regardless of browser support for native text fragments and even when the section is collapsed.)
- **Personal highlights and notes.** Each section can be highlighted and given a private margin note (hover a section for the "highlight" and "note" controls). These are stored in the browser's `localStorage` and persist across sessions on that device and browser — there is no account or server, so they are private to that browser and are not shared by a deep link. A toolbar shows how many sections you have marked, lets you filter to just those, and offers "Back up" / "Restore" to download all annotations as a JSON file and re-import them (so notes can survive a browser-data wipe or move to another device). Highlights and notes are also included in the text Export.
- **Search syntax.** Beyond plain keywords, the search box accepts `"quoted phrases"` for an exact match, `-word` to exclude a term, and `OR` between terms to match either. Phrases and exclusions combine with the relevance ranking and the title/full-text scope toggle.
- **Amendment timeline.** A section with Local Law amendments in two or more distinct years shows a small inline timeline of those enactment years (drawn from the same Local Law citations used for the amendment badge and sorts).
- **Export the current view.** The "Export" button downloads the sections currently shown — after any search, filter and sort — as a plain-text file, with a header recording the exact selection (search terms, filters, sort), the currency note, and the source/disclaimer. Each section carries its citation, chapter, any Charter Revision Commission changes, Local Laws on record and full text.
- **Limitations.** This is a reformatting of a third-party compilation, not the official code. Textual errors or omissions in the source carry through. For research and informational purposes only — not legal advice. Verify against the official code before relying on any provision.
